System dynamics involves understanding how complex systems change over time. It focuses on internal feedback loops and time delays that affect the behavior of the entire system. These concepts are crucial for moving beyond static analysis, as touched upon in Chapter 2 regarding interconnectedness.

3.1.1 Feedback Loops

Feedback loops can be either reinforcing (positive) or balancing (negative). Identifying and understanding these loops is key to predicting system behavior and designing effective interventions. Examples include:

  1. Population growth (reinforcing loop).
  2. Thermostat temperature regulation (balancing loop).
  3. Market speculation bubbles (reinforcing followed by potential collapse).
